Observed hydrological changes associated with active tectonic blocks before three consecutive earthquakes in Qinghai, China

Highlights:

1.        The hydrological changes observed in Qinghai province correlate well with the earthquakes in this region in the past 2 years.

2.        We calculated the relative amplitudes of the pre-seismic changes to explore the performances of the hydrological observations.

3.        The relative amplitudes of the pre-seismic changes are strongly modulated by the active tectonic blocks in Qinghai province.

 

Abstract

In the past 2 years, three earthquakes of magnitude 6.0 and above occurred consecutively in Qinghai province, China, i.e., the 22 May 2021 Ms7.4 Maduo, 8 January 2022 Ms6.9 Menyuan, and 26 March 2022 Ms6.0 Delingha earthquakes. The hydrological observation instruments set up by the China Earthquake Administration allow us to study the dynamic processes in the well-aquifer systems during the establishment of criticality. Particularly, the observations played an important role in the prediction of the 8 January 2022 Ms6.9 Menyuan earthquake which was approved by the people’s Government of Qinghai province. This work presents the hydrological data recorded by 7 stations to show the short-term anomalies before these earthquakes. To explore the performances of the hydrological observations in detecting earthquakes that occurred on different active tectonic blocks, we calculate the relative amplitudes of the pre-seismic changes. Results indicate that markedly pre-seismic change is found if the observation station and the detection earthquake are on the same block, and moderate change is found if they are on the adjacent blocks, while the precursor is hard to be identified if they are on the separated blocks. The variations in the hydrological responses may be caused by the strength weakening (or dilatancy) of source media. And the increased volumes in the crust can be evidenced by the changes in the geodetic time series in the same neighborhoods and during the same period, augmenting stress loading between the blocks.

 

Keywords: Hydrological observation; pre-seismic change; relative amplitude; active tectonic block; geodetic time series.

 

Introduction

Earthquake prediction in China is mainly based on empirical methods, in which nationwide observation stations are set up to monitor earthquake-related information. On 8 January 2022, the Ms6.9 Menyuan earthquake (MYEQ) occurred in Qinghai province, China. The earthquake fell in the annual seismic hazard regions for earthquakes of magnitude 6.0 and above in China in 2022 (Yu et al., 2022), and was detected by the China Earthquake Administration (CEA) on a short-term time scale. The Qinghai earthquake agency, CEA submitted reports to the people’s Government of Qinghai province on December 30 and 31, 2021. They predicted that an earthquake of magnitude 6-7 would occur in Qinghai soon, and suggested measures to deal with the earthquake. This is a meaningful practice, in which the hydrological observations in Qinghai province played an important role.

Earthquake-related hydrological observations can reveal the change of regionally tectonic activities and indicate the stress state of source media as well as the assessment of the potential of a future earthquake (Martinelli et al., 2021). Many studies of earthquake precursor monitoring are carried out based on this thought. For example, Roeloffs (1988) summarized the hydrologically pre-seismic changes of earthquakes in China. Kissin et al. (1996) and King et al. (2008) reported respectively the short-term precursors in Turkmenistan and Japan. Skelton et al. (2014) showed the changes in hydrochemistry before the earthquakes in Iceland. Moreover, Cicerone et al. (2009) suggested that the amplitude of a hydrologically pre-seismic anomaly is decided by the magnitude of the detection earthquake and distance from the epicenter (Mogi et al., 1989; Yan et al., 2018). However, we found that the changes before the MYEQ are hard to be explained just by using this magnitude-space relationship between the observation station and an earthquake of magnitude 6-7. Marked differences exist in the amplitudes of the changes between the stations which are not far from each other. In particular, besides the MYEQ, the 22 May 2021 Ms7.4 Maduo earthquake (MDEQ), and 26 March 2022 Ms6.0 Delingha earthquake (DLHEQ) also occurred in Qinghai province. The comparison of the performances of the hydrological observations before these earthquakes makes the problem stand out more clearly.

Recent studies have indicated that the accumulation of tectonic stress is one of the major mechanisms to create hydrological changes (Roelofs, 1996; Shi et al., 2015; Yan et al., 2016). A large earthquake is usually regarded as the rupture of the lithosphere in the upper crust subjected to long-term tectonic stress (Schwartz and coppersmith, 1984; Ellsworth, 2013). If the source media are loaded to the critical state, an earthquake may occur (Townsend and Zoback, 2000). During the establishment of criticality, the permeability of source media increases with the development of cracks (Kinoshita et al., 2015; Shi et al., 2018; Yehuda and Charles, 2003). Since the depth of groundwater can reach 15-20km (Muir-Wood and King, 1993; Gudmundsson et al., 2001), the variation of permeability in the crust may lead to anomalous fluctuation in the hydrological system (Sun et al., 2020; Yu et al., 2021). Thus, the hydrologically pre-seismic changes are determined by the stress state of source media which is closely related to regional active tectonics (Irfan et al., 2020).

The physical and mechanical heterogeneities in each of the active tectonics in China have been delineated by the active tectonic blocks (Zhang et al., 2003) which were derived from the spatial distribution of strong earthquakes and GPS velocity fields (Wang et al., 2001; Wang and Shen, 2020). Based on the active tectonic block theory (Zheng et al., 2022), the formation mechanism of strong earthquakes has been established (Zhang and Zhang, 1999, 2000). The stress loading between the blocks is not only from the movement of blocks but also from their deformation (England and Molnar, 1997).

This work attempts to study the performances of the hydrologically pre-seismic changes associated with the MDEQ, MYEQ, and DLHEQ and their relationship with the active tectonic blocks in Qinghai province. To show the validity of the results, the geodetic time series observed in the same neighborhoods and during the same period are also displayed. This paper includes seven sections. In addition to the introduction section, the “Regional tectonic setting” section shows the regional active tectonics of the study area; the “Hydrological observations” section is about the observation stations and the changes before the MDEQ, MYEQ, and DLHEQ, the “Methods” section introduces the methodology for relative amplitude calculation of the pre-seismic changes, the “Model of hydrological response” section suggests the models of the hydrological responses to earthquakes occurring on different blocks, and in the “Discussion” and “Conclusions” we will present some discussions and conclusions.

 

Regional tectonic setting

Qinghai province is located on the northeastern margin of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau. GPS velocity measurements show that, due to the intensive extrusion of the Indian plate and the interaction between the Qinghai-Tibet plateau, Alxa block, and Ordos block, Qinghai province mainly moves along the NEE direction (Wang and Shen, 2020). The velocity decreases significantly from southwest to northeast, with a maximum of 25.0 mm/a at the southwest and a minimum of 10.02mm/a at the east (figure 1). In terms of the study of Zhang et al. (2003), Qinghai province can be divided into the Bayan Har, Qaidam, Qilian, and Qiangtang blocks (Figure 2). Because there is no earthquake-related hydrological observation on the Qiangtang block, the block is not taken into consideration in this study.

Figure 1

 

 

The southernmost block is the Bayan Har block, which moves along the direction of NE61o with a velocity of 21mm/a. The southern and northern boundaries of this block are the Mani-Yushu-Xianshuihe and eastern Kunlun faults, respectively, which are all dominated by the sinistral strike-slip. The eastern boundary is the Longmenshan fault zone with a thrust velocity of 0.6-1.2mm/a (Densmore et al., 2007; Wang et al., 2014), and the western boundary is composed of the southwest segment of the Altyn fault. On this block, the 14 November 2001 Ms8.1 HohXil, 21 March 2008 Ms7.3 Yutian, 12 May 2008 Ms8.0 Wenchuan, 14 April 2010 Ms7.1 Yushu, 20 April 2013 Ms7.0 Lushan, 12 February 2014 Ms7.3 Yutian, 8 August 2017 Ms7.0 Jiuzhaigou, and 22 May 2021 Ms7.4 Maduo earthquakes occurred.

Figure 2

 

 

The north side of the Bayan Har block is the Qaidam block. The seismic activity on this block is markedly lower than that of the Bayan Har block, with an average of 0.15 earthquakes of magnitude 6.0 or above every year. The movement direction of the Qaidam block is the same as the Bayan Har block, with an average velocity of 12-14 mm/a.

The northernmost block is the Qilian orogenic belt. This block moves almost in the direction of EW, with a speed of 7-14 mm/a. The MYEQ and DLHEQ occurred on this block.

 

Hydrological observations

In the past 2 years, the MDEQ, MYEQ, and DLHEQ occurred consecutively in Qinghai province. Fortunately, there are equipped with high-quality hydrological observation instruments, allowing us to study the dynamic responses in the hydrological system before and after the earthquakes. Figure 2 shows the location of the observation stations. They are distributed on the Qilian, Qaidam, and Bayan Har blocks, respectively. Detailed information about the stations is listed in Table 1. Figures 3 and 4 show the hydrological data recorded by each of the stations, including the groundwater temperature at Gonghe, Delingha, and Yushu, the groundwater level at Zuoshu, and Pingan, and the gaseous radon at Xining, and Ledu.

In figure 3a, the Gonghe groundwater temperature dropped rapidly by 0.24℃ in March 2021, about 60 days before the MDEQ, with an epicenter distance of 280km. Then, in December of the same year, a smaller decrease of 0.1℃ was observed again, and the MYEQ occurred 25 days later, with an epicenter distance of 180km. Thereafter, in February 2022, about 30 days before the DLHEQ, the temperature sharply dropped (~0.05℃) for the third time, with an epicenter distance of 380km.

Similar anomalies before the MDEQ, MYEQ, and DLHEQ could also be found in the time series of the Zuoshu groundwater level (figure 3b). It dropped by 0.27m on 25 February 2021, 86 days before the MDEQ, with a distance of 372km; 0.93m on 25 August 2021, 136 days before the MYEQ and 132km from the epicenter; and 0.47m on 10 March 2022, 16 days before the DLHEQ, with the distance of 420km.

Figure 3

 

 

There were two significant reductions in the time series of the Xining gaseous radon, both of which exceeded 20 Bq/L (figure 3c). One is 61 days before the MYEQ, and the other occurred 15 days before the DLHEQ. The distances to the epicenter of these two earthquakes are 133 and 450 kilometers, respectively. In addition, the Ledu gaseous radon, Delingha groundwater temperature, and Pingan groundwater level showed uplift or anomalous annual variation about 94, 37, and 89 days before the MYEQ (figures 3d-f), with the epicenter distances of 169, 344, and 160 kilometers. The Yushu groundwater temperature dropped by 0.0038℃ on 15 March 2021, about 32 days before the MDEQ (figure 4e), with an epicenter distance of 214km.

Methods

Comparing the hydrological data observed by different stations (figures 3 and 4), it is clear that they all detected markedly pre-seismic anomalies, such as the changes in the Gonghe groundwater temperature. The distances to the MDEQ, MYEQ, and DLHEQ are respectively 180, 280, and 380 kilometers, and the corresponding amplitudes of pre-seismic changes are 0.24, 0.1, and 0.05 degrees. We also note that the magnitudes of these earthquakes are 7.4, 6.9, and 6.0. The observation results seem to correlate well with the magnitude-space relationship between the station and the mainshock (figure 3a). More detailed comparison of the pre-seismic changes, however, we notice a significant difference. Although the Zuoshu station is located just about 120 kilometers east of the Gonghe (figure 2), the amplitudes of the observed changes before the MDEQ, MYEQ, and DLHEQ, which are respectively 0.27, 0.93, and 0.47 meters (figure 3b), are different from that of the Gonghe groundwater temperature. The amplitude before the MDEQ is not only lower than that before the MYEQ but also lower than that before the DLHEQ, even though the distance to the MDEQ (372km) is shorter than that to the DLHEQ (420km).

Figure 5 shows the relative amplitudes derived from the pre-seismic changes displayed in figures 3 and 4. Since the Delingha groundwater temperature, Pingan groundwater level, and Ledu gaseous radon exhibited anomalies just only before the MYEQ, it is unnecessary to include their relative amplitudes.

Before the MDEQ, MYEQ, and DLHEQ, the relative amplitudes produced by the Gonghe groundwater temperature are 6.0, 2.5, and 1.25, and those produced by the Zuoshu groundwater level are roughly 2.7, 9.3, and 4.7 (figure 5). For the detection of MYEQ, the relative amplitude of the Zuoshu groundwater level was much larger than that of the Gonghe groundwater temperature, while before the MDEQ, the situation is quite different. In addition, for some observations such as the Xining gaseous radon, significant anomalies could be observed before the MYEQ and DLHEQ, but could not be found before the MDEQ, even though the distance to the MDEQ (375km) is less than that to the DLHEQ (450km).

Figure 5

 

 

Apart from that, the Yushu groundwater temperature changed anomalously not only before the MDEQ but also, within a short time, before several other earthquakes that occurred on the Bayan Har block, such as the 12 May 2008 Ms8.0 Wenchuan, 14 April 2010 Ms7.1 Yushu, and 8 August 2017 Ms7.0 Jiuzhaigou earthquakes (figures 4b-d). Nevertheless, the MYEQ, whose magnitude and epicenter distance are almost the same as the Jiuzhaigou earthquake, was not detected by this station.

Tectonically, the hydrological observation stations discussed above are located on different active tectonic blocks (figure 2). The Yushu station is located on the Bayan Har block, while the Gonghe station is on the Qaidam block. Although the location of Zuoshu is very close to the Gonghe station, this station, as well as the Pingan, Ledu, and Delingha stations, is on the Qilian block. On the other hand, the detection earthquakes, i.e., the MDEQ, MYEQ, and DLHEQ, are also distributed on different blocks: the Bayan Har and Qilian blocks. According to the observations at each of the stations (figure 5), we can infer that the active tectonic blocks may have some impact on the performance of the hydrologically pre-seismic changes. The relative amplitude of the pre-seismic change is very significant when the observation station and the detection earthquake are on the same block; smaller amplitude is observed when they are on the adjacent blocks, and the precursor can hardly be identified when they are on the separated blocks.

 

Model of hydrological response

We attempt to establish a model to connect the performances of the hydrologically pre-seismic changes with earthquakes occurring on different blocks (Figure 6).

Figure 6

 

 

When an observation station is located on the Qilian block (figure 6a), it is very sensitive to earthquakes occurring on the same block. This can be approved by the significant anomalies before the MYEQ recorded by the Zuoshu groundwater level, Xining gaseous radon, Pingan groundwater level, Ledu gaseous radon, and Delingha groundwater temperature. Oppositely, the performance for predicting earthquakes that occurred on the Bayan Har block is low. We note that just the Zuoshu groundwater level manifested some short-term changes before the MDEQ (figure 3). Because the seismicity on the Qaidam block is relatively low, we just focused on earthquakes that occurred on the Qilian and Bayan Har blocks.

When the station is on the Qaidam block (figure 6b), it can detect earthquakes on its adjacent blocks (the Qilian and Bayan Har blocks) with moderate sensitivity (figure 5). The Gonghe groundwater temperature (figure 3a) showed a clear short-term anomaly not only before the earthquake that occurred on the Bayan Har block (the MDEQ) but also before the earthquake on the Qilian block (the MYEQ and DLHEQ). However, the relative amplitude of the Gonghe groundwater temperature, before the MYEQ (~2.5, figure 3a), is much smaller than that of the Zuoshu groundwater level (~9.3, figure 3b). Moreover, the relative amplitude of the Gonghe groundwater temperature, for the prediction of MDEQ (6.0, figure 3a), was lower than that of the Yushu groundwater temperature (7.6, figure 4e).

When the station is located on the Bayan Har block (figure 6c), such as the Yushu groundwater temperature, it can well detect earthquakes that occurred on the Bayan Har block (figure 4) but has virtually no response to earthquakes occurring on the Qilian block (figure 5).

Our model looks to correlate well with the hydrological observations presented in this study, however, we do not know how robust it is, because just limited data are available for the analysis. Especially on the Bayan Har block, just only the Yushu station keeps running. Although it is very sensitive to strong earthquakes that occurred on the Bayan Har block, the sensitivity to earthquakes occurring on the Qilian block is needed to be verified by more observations.

In addition, our results also indicate that the distance to the epicenter should be included in determining the relative amplitude of the pre-seismic changes. As shown in figure 5, when the observation station and the detection earthquake are on the same active tectonic block, such as the Zuoshu groundwater level and Xining gaseous radon before the MYEQ, Yushu groundwater temperature before the MDEQ, and Zuoshu groundwater level and Xining gaseous radon before the DLHEQ, the epicenter distances can be divided into three levels, i.e., 130, 210, and 420 kilometers, while the corresponding relative amplitudes are roughly 9.0, 7.5, and 5.0. Moreover, the Yushu groundwater temperature (figure 4) did not show any anomalous change before the 21 March 2008 Ms7.3 Yutian and 12 February 2014 Ms7.3 Yutian earthquakes with epicenter distances of 1440 and 1380 kilometers (figure 2). The relative amplitude decreases with the increased epicenter distance.

 

Discussion

The high-quality earthquake-related hydrological observations in Qinghai province allow us to study the short-term precursors before large earthquakes. Note that all the pre-seismic changes presented in this paper have been validated by the CEA immediately after the occurrence. By comparing the observations with precipitation and air pressure, the meteorological interferences were eliminated. And by investigating the surface water level, groundwater exploitation, agricultural irrigation, pollution, and other, artificial activities were excluded.

The temporal and spatial evolutions of the hydrological changes before the MDEQ, MYEQ, and DLHEQ (figures 3 and 4) are similar to the short-term precursors suggested by Scholz et al. (1973), which could be explained by using the stress-strain relationship of a rock system (figure. 7a, Wawersik and Brace, 1971). When the tectonic stress is low, i.e., the system is in the elastic stage, earthquakes are difficult to be triggered; when the tectonic stress is high, i.e., the system is in the phase of dilatancy, any small disturbance may trigger earthquakes. The observed hydrological responses, such as the decreases in the Gonghe groundwater temperature, the increases in the Ledu gaseous radon, and the anomalous annual variation in the Pingan groundwater level may be created by the variation of porosity and permeability in source media (Yu et al., 2021). In terms of the studies of Kawabata et al. (2020) and Woith et al. (2015), these changes can all be adopted as indicators for testing future large earthquakes.

Figure 7

 

 

The difference in the hydrological responses between the active tectonic blocks in Qinghai province can be explained by using the earthquake nucleation theory found by He et al. (2021) and Ma et al. (2022). Because of the complexity of tectonic stress and heterogeneity of source media, the stress accumulation in the strength weakening area in each of the blocks is inconsistent. Some of them might be critically loaded (where earthquakes tend to occur), while others are not, leading to the difference in the variation of porosity and permeability, as well as the hydrological responses, between the blocks.

On the other hand, during the preparation for an earthquake, the block which is critically loaded may have some influence on its surrounding blocks. If we assume that block A (this assumption is equally valid for blocks B and C) is critically loaded (figure 7a), in addition to the force generated by block motion (figure 7b), the extra force derived from the deformation of the block caused by the dilatancy or strength weakening (as the variation of strain increments from point 1 to point 2) augment the stresses in its adjacent blocks (figure 7c). Thereby, the pre-seismic changes may also be recorded by the stations on these blocks with the increased stresses, such as the changes in the Gonghe groundwater temperature before the MDEQ, MYEQ, and DLHEQ. However, the sensitivity of hydrological response should be decreased due to the friction and energy dissipation between the blocks. This is even more serious for the separated blocks, such as blocks A and C. Among the stations on the Qilian block, just only the Zuoshu groundwater level manifested some anomalies before the MDEQ (which is located on the Bayan Har block), while for the stations on the Bayan Har block, such as the Yushu groundwater temperature, the precursor can hardly be found before the earthquakes that occurred on the Qilian block (e.g., the MYEQ). Knowing the unique characteristic of the tectonics-related hydrological changes, we may adopt that for detecting more earthquakes in the future.

Finally, we show that the increased crustal deformation before the mainshocks could be evidenced by the changes in the geodetic time series observed in the same neighborhoods and during the same period (figure 8). The Gaotai Borehole tilt, which is on the north side of the Qilian block (figure 2), was accelerated to tilt northward before the MYEQ and DLHEQ (figure 8a). The Huangyuan borehole strain, which is located south of the Qilian block, was compressed sharply months before the earthquakes on both Bayan Har and Qilian blocks (e.g., the MDEQ and MYEQ).

Conclusions

We revealed that the hydrological changes observed in Qinghai province correlate well with the earthquakes in this region in recent years. This observation is valuable for exploring hydrological responses during the seismogenic process. By using the empirical method of short-term precursor monitoring, the potential of future large earthquakes within a certain spatial scope may be assessed. More importantly, we found that the performances of the pre-seismic changes are strongly modulated by the active tectonic blocks. This result augments the understanding of the stress interaction between active tectonic blocks during the preparation for a large earthquake, which is constructive for the countries such as China that are carrying out earthquake prediction tasks. By using this strategy, in practice, we can evaluate the potential of a future earthquake with much more confidence. Meanwhile, it is possible to estimate earthquake location by analyzing earthquake-related precursory information through hydrological observation networks.

Figure 6. Model of hydrological response of an observation station to earthquakes occurring on different blocks. (a): The observation station is on the Qilian block. Red represents the earthquake that occurred on the Qilian block. The sensitivity of the response is high. Light blue denotes the earthquake that occurred on the Bayan Har block, and the sensitivity is low. (b): The observation station is on the Qaidam block. Yellow means the earthquake that occurred on the Qilian or Bayan Har blocks. Moderate sensitivity is observed. (c): The observation station is on the Bayan Har block. Red: the earthquake occurred on the Bayan Har block. Light gray: the earthquake occurred on the Qilian block, which is hardly detected by this station. White is for the earthquake that occurred on the Qaidam block, the sensitivity, however, is unknown.

 

Figure 7.

Figure 7. Schematic diagram of the influences of block A on its surrounding blocks during the preparation for an earthquake. (a): The constitutive law of a rock, depicting the stress-strain evolution of block A. The strain increment derived from a small increment of stress at the stage of dilatancy or strength weakening is greater than that at the elasticity. (b): Block A is in the elastic state. The stresses between the blocks are derived from their movements. (c): Block A is in the dilatancy or strength weakening process. The crustal deformation of block A enhances stresses in its surrounding blocks. This effect is equally valid for an earthquake occurring on block B or C.
